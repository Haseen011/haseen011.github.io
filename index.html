01 — About

A researcher's-eye view of reaction engineering

I'm a chemical engineer working at the interface of heterogeneous catalysis and reaction kinetics, currently a Research Scientist at Center of Excellence in Oil, Gas, and Energy (CoEOGE), Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IIT Bombay). My Ph.D. at IIT Bombay focused on understanding structure–activity relationships in supported metal catalysts, and I've since carried that mechanistic lens into applied process design — asking not just why a reaction pathway works, but how to run it reliably outside a fume hood.

I care about work that survives contact with a pilot plant: kinetics that hold up at scale, characterization that explains deactivation before it happens, and models that engineers other than me can actually use.

02 — Research

What I work on

Heterogeneous Catalysis

Design and characterization of supported metal and metal-oxide catalysts — including amine-doped Cu systems for CO₂-to-methanol and catalysts for ethylene oligomerization.

Reaction Kinetics & Mechanism

Kinetic studies and mechanistic analysis to resolve rate-determining steps and deactivation pathways across catalytic systems.

Process Scale-Up

Bridging lab-scale kinetics and pilot-scale reactor design — mass/heat transfer effects, catalyst lifetime, and techno-economics.

Sustainable Process Design

Route selection and catalyst design for lower energy-intensity chemical manufacturing, including CO₂ utilization pathways.

Biomass Gasification

Experimental and modeling studies of gasification processes, with a focus on high-ash biomass feedstocks.

Molecular & Kinetic Modeling

Computational studies — such as DFT-based catalyst screening and reaction pathway analysis — used to guide catalyst and process design.

Process Simulation

Flowsheet simulation and simulator development, such as equation-oriented modeling for units like a CDU simulator.
